✨ First Day...

Ganesh Chaturthi marks the birth of Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles and the god of wisdom. On the first day, devotees bring home beautifully crafted idols of Ganesha and place them on decorated platforms called pandals. The installation ceremony includes prayers, chanting of mantras, and offerings of sweets like modaks, Ganesha’s favorite. Special rituals like Prana Pratishtha invoke the divine presence into the idol. The first day signifies new beginnings, devotion, and the start of a ten-day festival filled with worship and celebration.

🎉 Second Day...

Ganesh Chaturthi is a vibrant Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, the god of wisdom and remover of obstacles. It begins with installing Ganesha idols in homes and public places, followed by prayers and offerings, especially sweets like modak, his favorite. The festival lasts for 10 days and concludes with the immersion of the idol in water, symbolizing Ganesha’s return to the divine world.

💃🕺 Last Day...

"As we bid farewell to Bappa, let’s cherish the joy-filled memories. May Ganpati shower blessings and remove all obstacles from our lives. With love in our hearts, we celebrate until He returns next year!"

👕 Tshirt ...

"Sri Sri Sri Varasiddhi Vinayaka Puchavani Thotalu" Sri Sri Sri Varasiddhi Vinayaka: This is a respectful way of addressing Lord Ganesha, particularly referencing the renowned Varasiddhi Vinayaka deity. Puchavani Thotalu: This is likely the name of a locality, colony, or group associated with the community celebration or organizing committee.

The last day of Ganesh Chaturthi, Anant Chaturdashi, is filled with devotion and emotions. Families perform the final prayers, offering flowers and sweets to Lord Ganesha. Devotees carry beautifully decorated idols in lively processions, singing and dancing through the streets. The idols are immersed in water, symbolizing a heartfelt farewell to the Lord. The day ends with gratitude, joy, and cherished memories of the festival.😊
